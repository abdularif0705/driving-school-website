Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Your G1, G2, and G Driver's Licence

Published on July 23, 2024

Getting your Ontario driver’s license is an exciting milestone, but the process can be confusing for many new drivers. Ontario follows a Graduated Licensing System (GLS) consisting of three stages: G1, G2, and G tests. Each stage comes with its own test, requirements, restrictions, and fees.

In this complete guide, we’ll walk you through the entire process—from understanding the rules and costs to expert tips on how to pass each test on your first try.

Rajput Driving School (Windsor) is MTO-approved and offers BDE, G2/G prep, and mock test coaching. If you want structured lessons or a car for your road test, book with us or see our courses.

Understanding Ontario’s Graduated Licensing System (GLS)

Ontario’s GLS is designed to help beginner drivers gain experience gradually before earning full driving privileges. Here’s a breakdown of each stage in the Ontario driver’s license journey:

  • G1 License (Learner’s Permit) – Requires passing a written test on traffic rules and road signs.
  • G2 License (First Road Test) – Allows independent driving with some restrictions.
  • G License (Full License) – The final road test removes most driving restrictions.

Each stage comes with specific rules, costs, and requirements, which we’ll explain below.

1) Ontario Driver’s License: G1 Stage — Rules, Cost & How to Prepare

What is the G1 Test?

The G1 test is a written, multiple-choice exam taken on a computer at a DriveTest Centre. It assesses your knowledge of:

  • Road signs
  • Traffic laws
  • Safe driving practices

The test is available in multiple languages.

G1 Test Rules

  • Be at least 16 years old.
  • Pass a vision test.
  • Pass the written knowledge test.

Once you have your G1, you must:

  • Always drive with a fully licensed G driver (4+ years of experience) in the front passenger seat.
  • Maintain a zero blood alcohol level (0.00 BAC).
  • Avoid 400-series highways/high-speed expressways (unless with a licensed instructor).
  • Not drive between midnight and 5 a.m.

G1 Test Cost

$159.75 package covers: G1 knowledge test, first G2 road test attempt, and 5-year licence validity. Retest fee: $16.00.

Top Tips to Pass the G1 Test

  • Study the Ontario Driver’s Handbook.
  • Take online practice tests (timed) to learn the format.
  • Read questions carefully—watch for tricky wording.
  • Arrive early and stay relaxed.

What to Bring & Expect on G1 Test Day

  • Two pieces of ID, payment method, and glasses/contacts (if required).
  • Quick vision check at the counter; then you’ll sit the test on a computer.
  • Results are given immediately; rewrites are available (capacity permitting).

10 Common G1 Mistakes

  1. Skimming the handbook; missing details
  2. Not practicing with mock exams
  3. Rushing through questions
  4. Confusing similar signs
  5. Ignoring right-of-way nuances
  6. Miscalculating stopping distances
  7. Guessing without eliminating options
  8. Poor time/pace management
  9. Test anxiety (no breathing/reset strategy)
  10. No review of marked/unsure items

2) G2 License — Rules, Cost & How to Prepare

What is the G2 Road Test?

The G2 (G1 exit) test evaluates basic driving skills like smooth starts/stops, safe lane changes, intersections, and 3-point turns/parking.

G2 Rules

  • Hold G1 for 12 months (or 8 months with an MTO-approved BDE course).
  • 0.00 BAC if under 21; seatbelts required for all passengers under 16.
  • Driving on all roads, including highways, is allowed.

G2 Cost

Included in the $159.75 paid at G1. Retest: $53.75.

Top Tips to Pass the G2 Test

  • Practice in varied conditions (day/night, rain, traffic).
  • Master parallel parking & 3-point turns.
  • Mirror/blind-spot checks every maneuver.
  • Keep to the speed limit (no speeding or crawling).

3) G License — Rules, Cost & How to Prepare

What is the G Road Test?

The G (G2 exit) test assesses advanced skills: highway merges/exits, lane discipline, hazard response, and speed management.

G Rules

  • Hold G2 for at least 12 months.
  • 0.00 BAC if under 21.

G Cost

$91.25 (retest: $91.25).

Top Tips to Pass the G Test

  • Get comfortable with highway driving (merge, maintain, exit smoothly).
  • Keep both hands on the wheel; drive predictably.
  • Stay calm and obey all traffic laws/signals.

4) How to Book Your Ontario Driving Test

  • Online: DriveTest
  • Phone: 1-888-570-6110
  • In Person: Any DriveTest Centre

Tip: Book early—some locations have long wait times.

5) What to Bring on Your Test Day

  • Your G1/G2 licence (for identity)
  • Valid insurance and registration
  • A roadworthy vehicle (lights, brakes, mirrors, signals working)
  • Glasses/contacts if required

6) Common Mistakes That Cause Failing

  • Not checking mirrors and blind spots
  • Speeding or driving too slowly
  • Rolling stops at stop signs
  • Failing to yield right of way
  • Panic or over-hesitation

If you fail, don’t panic—reschedule and try again after the waiting period.

7) Final Thoughts

Earning your Ontario driver’s license is a step-by-step process, but preparation is key. By studying the rules, practicing consistently, and following the tips in this guide, you can confidently pass your G1, G2, and G driving tests.

Good luck, and drive safely!
