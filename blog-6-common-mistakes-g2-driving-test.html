Common Mistakes People Make on Their G2 Driving Test

Published on June 24, 2024

Passing the G2 driving test is a significant step towards obtaining your full driver’s license in Ontario. However, many learners make common mistakes that can lead to failing the test. Understanding these mistakes and how to avoid them can greatly increase your chances of success.

In this article, we’ll explore some of the most common errors people make during their G2 driving test and provide tips on how to avoid them.

1. Not Checking Blind Spots

The Mistake: Failing to Check Blind Spots: One of the most common mistakes is not checking blind spots when changing lanes or merging. This oversight can be dangerous and is a critical error during the test.

How to Avoid It: Always Check Blind Spots: Make it a habit to check your blind spots by looking over your shoulder before changing lanes or merging. Practice this regularly during your driving lessons to make it second nature.

2. Improper Lane Changes

The Mistake: Incorrect Lane Changes: Many learners fail to signal properly, change lanes too quickly, or do not check mirrors and blind spots before changing lanes.

How to Avoid It: Signal and Check: Always use your turn signal well in advance of changing lanes. Check your mirrors and blind spots before making the move. Change lanes smoothly and gradually.

3. Rolling Stops

The Mistake: Rolling Through Stop Signs: Another common mistake is not coming to a complete stop at stop signs. Rolling stops can result in an automatic fail.

How to Avoid It: Full Stop: Ensure you come to a complete stop at all stop signs. Wait for at least three seconds before proceeding, and check for other vehicles and pedestrians.

4. Speeding or Driving Too Slowly

The Mistake: Incorrect Speed: Driving either too fast or too slow can be problematic. Exceeding the speed limit or driving significantly below it can both result in lost points.

How to Avoid It: Maintain Proper Speed: Always adhere to the posted speed limits. Practice maintaining a consistent speed that matches the flow of traffic while staying within legal limits.

5. Poor Observation Skills

The Mistake: Lack of Observation: Failing to observe and react to road signs, signals, and other road users is a common error. This includes not noticing pedestrians, cyclists, or other vehicles.

How to Avoid It: Stay Alert: Keep your eyes moving and scan the road ahead, as well as your mirrors. Be aware of your surroundings and anticipate potential hazards.

6. Improper Turns

The Mistake: Incorrect Turning Techniques: Making wide turns, cutting corners, or not signaling properly during turns are frequent mistakes.

How to Avoid It: Practice Turns: Practice making smooth, controlled turns. Signal well in advance, slow down before the turn, and ensure you stay within your lane throughout the maneuver.

7. Inadequate Following Distance

The Mistake: Tailgating: Following the vehicle in front of you too closely can be dangerous and is a common mistake during the test.

How to Avoid It: Maintain Safe Distance: Keep a safe following distance, typically a minimum of three seconds behind the vehicle in front of you. Increase this distance in adverse weather conditions.

8. Nervousness and Lack of Confidence

The Mistake: Test Anxiety: Nervousness can lead to mistakes such as jerky movements, hesitation, or forgetting basic driving rules.

How to Avoid It: Stay Calm: Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing before and during the test. Confidence comes with practice, so ensure you get plenty of driving experience before the test.

9. Not Following Examiner’s Instructions

The Mistake: Ignoring Instructions: Failing to follow the examiner’s instructions or misunderstanding them can lead to mistakes.

How to Avoid It: Listen Carefully: Pay close attention to the examiner’s instructions. If you’re unsure about something, don’t hesitate to ask for clarification.

10. Poor Parking Skills

The Mistake: Parking Errors: Struggling with parallel parking, reverse parking, or parking too far from the curb are common issues.

How to Avoid It: Practice Parking: Spend extra time practicing different parking maneuvers. Use cones or markers to simulate real parking situations and improve your skills.

Conclusion

Passing the G2 driving test requires preparation, practice, and attention to detail. By understanding and avoiding these common mistakes, you can increase your chances of success. Remember, the goal is not just to pass the test but to become a safe and responsible driver. Good luck on your journey to obtaining your G2 license!
