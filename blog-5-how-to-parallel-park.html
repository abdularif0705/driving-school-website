How to Parallel Park in 3 Easy Steps

Published on June 24, 2024

Parallel parking can be a daunting task for many drivers, especially new ones. However, with the right technique and a bit of practice, it can become a straightforward maneuver. At Rajput Driving School, we believe in simplifying complex driving tasks into easy-to-follow steps. Here’s our 3-step method to help you master parallel parking.



Why Do People Say Parallel Parking is Hard?

Parallel parking requires a high level of spatial awareness and precise control over your vehicle. Here are a few reasons why it can be challenging:

  • Spatial Awareness: Judging distances and angles accurately is crucial.
  • Narrow Spaces: Parallel parking spaces are often tight, leaving little room for error.
  • Stressful Environments: Busy streets with traffic and pedestrians can add pressure.
  • Lack of Practice: Many drivers don't practice parallel parking regularly, making it seem harder when they need to do it.

Despite these challenges, with practice and confidence, parallel parking can become a manageable and even routine task.

The 3-Step Method

Step 1: Signal and Align

  • Signal Right: Before you start, signal right to indicate your intention to park.
  • Pull Up Alongside: Position your car approximately 2 feet away from the car you want to park behind. Align your rear bumper with the other car's rear bumper. For vehicles of similar size, align mirror to mirror.

Step 2: Reverse and Angle

  • Put Car into REVERSE: Shift your car into reverse gear.
  • Turn Wheels Right: Turn your steering wheel one full turn to the right (the same direction as your signal).
  • Check Surroundings: Check your left mirror and left blind spot. Look out the back window over your right shoulder.
  • Back Up Slowly: If traffic allows, slowly back up until your car is at a 45-degree angle. You should see your right mirror between you and the other vehicle’s tail light. Stop at this point.

Step 3: Straighten and Park

  • Turn Wheels Left: Turn your steering wheel all the way to the left.
  • Back Up Slowly: Continue to back up slowly until your car is parallel with the curb. Use your right door mirror to ensure the curb and the right side of your car are parallel.
  • Straighten Wheels: Put your car in drive, turn the steering wheel 1.5 turns or until your wheels are straight. Move forward slowly until you are about 3 feet from the car in front of you, ensuring your wheels are straight.
  • Park: Put your vehicle in park. If done correctly, you should be less than 12 inches from the curb.

Practice will improve your judgment.

Tips When Choosing a Parking Spot

  • Space Selection: Choose a space that is at least six feet longer than your car.
  • Signal Early: Flash your brake lights and put on your turn signal before you pull up.
  • Check Traffic: Always look back to check traffic before backing up.
  • Take It Slow: Ease into the spot. Jumping the curb will cause you to fail your road test, but bumping the curb will not.

Exiting a Parallel Parking Space

  • Back Up: Reverse straight about 2 feet without touching the car behind. If you can see the rear tires below the bumper of the car in front of you, that is far enough.
  • Signal and Turn: Put your car in drive, signal left, then turn the wheels all the way to the left. Check your left door rear view mirror and then your left blind spot.
  • Pull Away: If clear, pull away slowly while turning your wheel back in the direction you want to go.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Parallel parking can be tricky, and common mistakes include misjudging the distance to the curb, hitting other cars, or not aligning correctly. Here are some tips to avoid these errors:

  • Practice Regularly: The more you practice, the better you will become.
  • Use Reference Points: Use your mirrors and reference points in your car to judge distances.
  • Stay Calm: Take a deep breath and stay focused. Don’t let the pressure of traffic or pedestrians rush you.

Why is Parallel Parking Important?

Parallel parking is an essential skill for several reasons:

  • City Driving: In busy cities, parallel parking is often the only option.
  • Driving Tests: You must demonstrate your ability to parallel park to pass your driving test.
  • Efficiency: It helps you fit into smaller spaces, making it easier to find parking.

Summary

Parallel parking doesn't have to be stressful. By following these three simple steps and practicing regularly, you can become proficient at parallel parking. Remember to always signal your intentions, check your surroundings, and take it slow. Happy parking from Rajput Driving School!
