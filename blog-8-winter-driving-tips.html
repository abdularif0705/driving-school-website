Winter Driving Safety: 10 Essential Tips for Navigating Snowy Roads

Published on June 24, 2024

As the temperature drops and snowflakes start to fall, driving conditions can become treacherous. Whether you're a seasoned driver or new to winter weather, it's crucial to adapt your driving habits to stay safe on snowy roads. This comprehensive guide will provide you with essential tips to navigate winter driving challenges confidently and safely.

1. Prepare Your Vehicle for Winter

The Tip: Ensure your car is winter-ready before the snow hits.

How to Do It:

  • Install winter tires for better traction and control
  • Check your battery, as cold weather can reduce its efficiency
  • Ensure your windshield wipers are in good condition and filled with winter-grade washer fluid
  • Keep your gas tank at least half full to prevent fuel line freezing and add weight to the vehicle
  • Have your vehicle serviced to check brakes, belts, hoses, and fluid levels
  • Test your car's heating and defrosting systems to ensure they're working properly
  • Consider using a fuel treatment product to prevent diesel from gelling (for diesel vehicles)

2. Assemble a Winter Survival Kit

The Tip: Keep essential items in your vehicle for emergencies.

How to Do It:

  • Include a charged phone and portable charger
  • Pack water and non-perishable food
  • Bring a flashlight with extra batteries
  • Keep blankets and warm clothes
  • Include jumper cables, a shovel, and an ice scraper
  • Pack traction mats or sand
  • Bring candles and a lighter or matches
  • Include a first-aid kit

3. Check Weather and Road Conditions

The Tip: Stay informed about current conditions before and during your trip.

How to Do It:

  • Check the weather forecast and delay your trip if conditions look bad
  • Visit Ontario 511 (ontario.ca/511) to check road conditions and find out where plows are on provincial highways
  • Plan your route in advance and know where safe stopping points are located

4. Adjust Your Driving Techniques

The Tip: Adapt your driving to winter conditions.

How to Do It:

  • Increase your following distance to at least 8-10 seconds
  • Brake gently and early, applying steady pressure
  • Accelerate and decelerate slowly to maintain traction
  • Avoid sudden movements, including quick lane changes or abrupt braking
  • Steer gently on curves and in slippery conditions
  • If you start to skid, steer in the direction you want to go
  • Don't use cruise control on wet, snowy, or icy pavement

5. Enhance Visibility

The Tip: Ensure you can see and be seen in winter conditions.

How to Do It:

  • Use your vehicle's full lighting system in poor visibility and whiteout conditions
  • Turn on your headlights to see and be seen by other drivers
  • Use low beams in heavy snow, as high beams can cause glare
  • Keep your lights clean and free of snow and ice
  • Consider using fog lights if visibility is severely reduced

Conclusion

Winter driving requires extra caution, preparation, and skill. By following these tips and adjusting your driving habits, you can significantly reduce your risk of accidents and stay safe on winter roads. Remember, the key to winter driving is to be patient, alert, and prepared for changing conditions.
