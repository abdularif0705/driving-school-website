What Happens If You Fail The G1 Test?

Published on July 24, 2024

The G1 knowledge test is a crucial step in obtaining your driver's license in Ontario, but it's not an easy one. Unless you've studied thoroughly or gone through a structured program, you may fail on your first attempt. Don't be discouraged, though, as 50-70% of students fail their first time!

Understanding the G1 test

The G1 written test is the first of three tests required to become a fully licensed driver in Ontario. It's the only written test - the other two (G2 and G) are in-car road tests.

Key points about the G1 test:

  • 40 total questions divided into two sections: Traffic Signs and Rules of the Road
  • You must score at least 80% (16/20) on each section to pass
  • If you fail one section but pass the other, you only need to retake the failed section
  • Takes about 20-30 minutes to complete
  • Available in multiple languages including English and French

Taking the G1 Test

You don't need to set up an appointment for your G1 test, as written tests are offered on a first-come, first-served basis. Here's the process:

  1. Walk into your local DriveTest centre and get in line.
  2. Take a ticket and get seated.
  3. When your name is called, head to the counter to fill out paperwork.
  4. Provide your ID, pay the fee ($159.75 for the G1 license package), and do a brief eye exam.
  5. Once paperwork is completed, go to the testing room.
  6. Take the test on a computer (or paper sheets in some locations).
  7. After finishing, return to the counter to receive your results.

What happens if you fail?

If you fail, you have two options:

  1. Retake the test immediately after reviewing your results
  2. Wait a few days before attempting it again

Regardless of which option you choose, you'll need to pay a $16 fee for every additional attempt.

Common reasons for failing the G1 test

Understanding why people fail can help you avoid the same pitfalls. Here are some common reasons:

  1. Insufficient preparation: Many test-takers underestimate the depth of knowledge required ESPECIALLY for the rules portion.
  2. Test anxiety: Nervousness can lead to silly mistakes or blanking out on known information.
  3. Misunderstanding questions: The wording of some questions can be tricky if you're not careful.
  4. Overlooking important details: People often overlook small but crucial details in traffic signs or rules.
  5. Rushing through the test: Taking your time to read and understand each question is crucial.

How to prepare for the G1 test

Doing practice tests is one of the most effective ways to prepare. While the MTO Handbook is a useful reference, it's not ideal as a practice tool. To increase your chances of passing, Rajput Driving School recommends:

  1. Doing as many mock exams as possible
  2. Practicing with questions on rules of the road, signs, and markings
  3. Using a structured G1 test prep course that includes practice questions and mock exams
  4. Studying the MTO Driver's Handbook thoroughly
  5. Focusing on areas where you consistently make mistakes in practice tests
  6. Discussing confusing topics with experienced drivers or instructors

Strategies for retaking the G1 test

If you do fail your first attempt, don't lose heart. Here are some strategies for your next try:

  1. Analyze your mistakes: Review which questions you got wrong and why.
  2. Focus on weak areas: Spend extra time studying the sections you struggled with.
  3. Take more practice tests: This will help build your confidence and familiarity with the test format.
  4. Consider a prep course: Structured learning can fill in knowledge gaps you might have missed.
  5. Manage test anxiety: Practice relaxation techniques to stay calm during the test.

What to do after passing the G1 test

After passing your G1:

  1. You must wait 12 months before taking the G2 road test
  2. Consider taking a Beginner Driver Education (BDE) course at an MTO-approved driving school like Rajput Driving School
  3. BDE courses reduce the G2 wait time by 4 months
  4. Most insurance companies offer 10-20% discounts for completing BDE

The standard BDE course includes:

  • 10 hours of in-vehicle training
  • 20 hours of online theory
  • 10 hours of flexible instruction

G1 restrictions and responsibilities

Once you have your G1 license, it's important to understand the restrictions:

  1. You must always drive with a fully licensed driver who has at least 4 years of driving experience.
  2. The accompanying driver must have a blood alcohol level of less than .05%.
  3. You cannot drive between midnight and 5 a.m.
  4. You cannot drive on 400-series highways or high-speed expressways.
  5. You must ensure that the number of seatbelts in the vehicle is equal to the number of passengers.

The path forward: G2 and G tests

After holding your G1 for 12 months (or 8 months with BDE), you'll be eligible for the G2 road test. This test assesses your ability to drive safely in typical road conditions. After passing the G2, you'll need to wait another 12 months before taking the final G road test, which includes highway driving.

Rajput Driving School provides comprehensive preparation for G1, G2 and G tests. Their certified driving instructors will help guide you through the licensing process, whether you use their online prep tools or receive training from them.

Remember, failing the G1 test is common and not the end of your driving journey. With proper preparation and persistence, you'll be on your way to getting fully licensed. Good luck!
